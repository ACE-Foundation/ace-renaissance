
# **Use Case 04 — ACE‑CERT (DACCS Integration)**  
### *From Systemic Necessity to Operational Implementation*

Use Case 03 demonstrated the systemic consequence of ACE’s unified metric:  
**biological CDR is finite, biomass‑bound, and insufficient to balance national‑scale emissions.**  
For Sweden, the biological ceiling is ≈22 Mt CO₂e per year, while national emissions remain ≈45 Mt CO₂e per year — leaving a **residual of ~23 Mt CO₂e** that cannot be closed by any biomass‑based pathway.

This residual is not a policy preference.  
It is a **mathematical boundary condition** revealed by ACE.

Use Case 04 shows how this residual is operationally addressed through **DACCS‑based ACE‑CERT**, and why Sweden is structurally advantaged in deploying it.

---

## **1. Why DACCS Fits Naturally into ACE**

ACE expresses all carbon removal — biological or technological — in the same unit: ton CO₂e

DACCS integrates seamlessly into this architecture because it provides:

- **pure, permanent CO₂ removals**  
- **no land use**  
- **no biomass dependency**  
- **full MRV compatibility**  
- **direct geological storage**  
- **cryptographically certifiable outputs**  

In ACE, DACCS is simply another CDR method with:

- zero hectares  
- zero biological pools  
- direct negative CO₂e  
- permanent storage  

This makes DACCS the **structural complement** to biological CDR.

---

## **2. Sweden’s Residual → Sweden’s DACCS Requirement**

From Use Case 03:

- Maximum biological CDR: **≈22 Mt CO₂e/yr**  
- National emissions: **≈45 Mt CO₂e/yr**  
- **Residual:**  
  
  45 - 22 = 23 Mt CO₂e/yr

This is the volume that must be covered by **DACCS‑based ACE‑CERT**.

ACE makes this requirement explicit, auditable and reproducible.

---

## **3. Energy Requirement for Sweden’s DACCS Residual**

DACCS typically requires:

- **1–3 MWh electricity per ton CO₂**  
- **2–6 MWh low‑temperature heat per ton CO₂**  

If heat is electrified:

3–5 MWh electricity per ton CO₂

For Sweden’s residual:

23 × (3 to 5) = 69 to 115 TWh electricity per year

This corresponds to:

- **≈1.0–1.7× Sweden’s annual hydropower production**  
- **≈40–70% of Sweden’s total electricity system**

ACE exposes this energy requirement with mathematical clarity.

---

## **4. Why Sweden Has a DACCS Sweet Spot**

Despite the large energy demand, Sweden is one of Europe’s **most favourable DACCS locations** because it uniquely combines:

### **A. Fossil‑free, stable electricity**
- **Hydropower**: 65–70 TWh/yr  
- **Nuclear baseload**  
- **Low marginal cost**  
- **Low carbon intensity**

DACCS requires stability — Sweden has it.

### **B. Cold climate**
Lower ambient temperatures reduce DACCS energy consumption.  
DACCS performs better in Sweden than in warmer regions.

### **C. Access to geological storage**
Sweden can utilize **North Sea storage formations**, already validated for CCS.

### **D. Large residual + strong energy base**
This combination creates a structural advantage:

- the residual is large enough to justify investment  
- the energy system is clean enough to make DACCS climate‑positive  
- the infrastructure is stable enough for year‑round operation  

ACE makes this advantage visible.

---

## **5. DACCS‑Based ACE‑CERT: Operational Flow**

DACCS integrates into ACE‑CERT through the same deterministic pipeline as biological CDR:

1. **Measurement**  
   – CO₂ captured from ambient air  
   – energy use logged  
   – storage pathway verified  

2. **Verification**  
   – MRV aligned with CRCF  
   – permanence validated  

3. **Certification**  
   – ACE‑CERT issued  
   – zero land use  
   – zero biological pools  
   – pure negative CO₂e  

4. **Matching**  
   – industrial ACE‑CERT (positive)  
   – DACCS ACE‑CERT (negative)  
   – deterministic balancing  

5. **Retirement**  
   – cryptographically sealed  
   – audit‑ready  
   – reproducible  

DACCS becomes the **long‑term backbone** of permanent removals in ACE.
